# Data Model

## Principles

- Do not define additional official rates beyond the working catalog rules confirmed by Kevin on 2026-06-22 unless an approved source is provided.
- Store monetary amounts as integer minor units, such as cents, with an explicit currency code.
- Keep totals reproducible from financial lines.
- Keep proposal versions immutable once sent or accepted.
- Validate dates, hours, quantities, required rates, and template language before approval.
- Keep seed data clearly fictitious.

## Core Entities

### TrainingPhase

Represents one phase inside a proposal version.

Suggested fields:

- `id`
- `proposalVersionId`
- `learnerIds`
- `startDate`
- `endDate`
- `trainingTypeId`
- `objectives`
- `deliveryMode`
- `schedule`
- `totalHours`
- `rateId`
- `description`
- `financialLineIds`

Each phase may have its own learners, dates, type, objectives, delivery mode, schedule, hours, rate, description, and price lines. Training start and end dates are required proposal information for federal-government clients and must remain visible in proposal review and generated content.

For the current proposal workflow, language-training schedule details and weekly rhythm are captured inside the selected second-language-training service block. Full-time rhythm uses 5 days per week with hours per day or hours per week. Individual training captures training start and end dates, then estimates hours from Monday-to-Sunday calendar weeks touched by the training date range and the weekly rhythm. An individual-training block defaults to 1 individual learner but may represent multiple individual learners when they share the same language, schedule, delivery mode, and duration; billable hours equal `individual learners x hours per learner`, including reserve hours per learner when used. Part-time group training captures one or more group sets. A group set may represent several identical groups and stores the number of groups, language, expected participants total, session, program start date, teaching weeks, buffer weeks, classes per week, class duration, and delivery mode. Availability and scheduling preferences are structured separately from billable-hour calculations and may include unavailable weekdays, preferred weekdays, AM or PM preference, time zone, location or delivery context, statutory-holiday handling, and additional notes. Saturday, Sunday, and statutory holidays are excluded by default for internal planning; default exclusions should not be repeated in the client proposal unless customized. Preferences apply to the whole service by default, but individual services with multiple learners may store preferences per learner and part-time group services may store preferences per group set. Expected participants describe the client request. By default this value is suggested from `number of groups x maximum participants per group`; if the user edits it manually, the manual value is preserved. Maximum participants per group describe the applicable capacity rule and must be calculated from the SOA or Price Book when available; otherwise use a default maximum of 6 per group. Teaching weeks drive billable hours. Buffer weeks extend the calendar planning span for holidays or scheduling risk, but do not increase billable hours by themselves. If pricing source, service type, class type, or training format changes, the request must become another service instead of another group set. Individual training may add reserve hours for client-requested funding coverage; group and semi-private training must not use reserve hours. A later detailed `TrainingPhase` model may expand this into multiple phases without moving dates away from the service being quoted.

### ServiceCatalogItem

Represents one selectable service that can appear in a proposal.

Working service families confirmed on 2026-06-22:

- Second language training;
- Diagnostic assessment;
- Assessment / evaluation / placement;
- Placement test;
- NMSO placement test;
- CCC assessment.

Suggested fields:

- `id`
- `code`
- `family`
- `labelEn`
- `labelFr`
- `descriptionEn`
- `descriptionFr`
- `contractVehicleType`
- `allowedDeliveryModes`
- `allowedTrainingFormats`
- `allowedClassTypes`
- `allowedCompetencies`
- `requiresAllCompetencies`
- `canCombineWithTraining`
- `isActive`

Important modelling rules:

- Second language training supports full-time and part-time formats, group and individual class types, and online, in-person, or hybrid delivery.
- Hybrid delivery is a delivery mode; it must not hide whether the training is full-time or part-time.
- Diagnostic assessment and CCC assessment must allow one, two, or three selected competencies from Reading, Writing, and Oral, and the generated description must name the selected competencies.
- `NMSO placement test` is a source-specific product label and must be used only for NMSO SOA lines.
- Other SOA placement, diagnostic, assessment, or evaluation lines must use source-appropriate generic service labels such as `Placement test` or `Assessment / evaluation / placement`.
- NMSO placement test must require all three competencies and may be added to a language-training proposal.
- Assessment and placement services use a per-candidate or per-selected-competency basis; they do not use training class type or rhythm.
- Assessment and placement services capture one or more assessment groups. Each group has a language, a candidate count, and a competency mix. This supports cases such as French oral only, French writing plus oral, English all competencies, and English oral only in the same proposal.
- Service descriptions must make the selected service, competencies, delivery mode, class type, and contractual source clear enough for review.
- Proposal service selection should start with the pricing path, `SOA` or `Non-SOA`, because client-requested revisions often switch between these paths.
- Available service type choices must be derived from the selected Price Book source. If an SOA has only language-training lines, the proposal editor must show only `Second language training` for that SOA.
- Compatible Price Book rates are selected automatically from the configured pricing path, SOA source, service type, class type, candidate quantity, competencies, delivery mode, and hours.
- Non-SOA proposal lines may store a proposal-specific rate override with a required adjustment note. This override belongs to the proposal line only and must not mutate the Price Book. SOA rates must remain locked to the selected active SOA line.
- The selected rate, source, unit, quantity, active dates, and estimated amount must remain visible and auditable before approval.
- Estimated proposal amounts are pre-tax prototype amounts until tax and final rounding rules are confirmed.
- Group training starts at 2 participants. No general maximum participant count applies unless the selected source explicitly states a cap.
- Do not capture an expected acceptance date during draft creation. The applicable rate is validated later, when the real acceptance or contract-received date is known.
- Standard offer-validity wording belongs in proposal templates. Current Knowledge Circle offers are valid for 30 days.

## Approval Validation

A proposal version can be approved internally only when:

- required client, contact, learner, phase, and language fields are valid;
- each required rate is present and active;
- every financial line has valid quantity and money fields;
- totals can be recalculated deterministically;
- the version is not immutable.

## Versioning Rule

If a sent or accepted version needs changes:

1. keep the existing version unchanged;
2. create a new `ProposalVersion`;
3. set `basedOnVersionId`;
4. mark the new version as a revision;
5. record the status change in history.

## Local Schema Versioning

IndexedDB must include a schema version. Migrations must be explicit and tested before changing stored structures.

Backup exports must include:

- export format version;
- application data version;
- exported timestamp;
- settings;
- proposals and versions;
- clients, contacts, and learners;
- training types and rates;
- attachments metadata;
- attachment payloads or documented attachment export handling.
